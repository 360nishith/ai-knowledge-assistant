Artificial intelligence (AI) is the ability of a digital computer or computer-controlled robot to perform tasks commonly associated with intelligent beings. The term is frequently applied to the project of developing systems endowed with intellectual processes characteristic of humans, such as reasoning, discovering meaning, generalizing, and learning from past experience. Since the development of digital computers in the 1940s, machines have been programmed to carry out highly complex tasks, including proving mathematical theorems and playing chess. Despite remarkable advances in processing power and memory capacity, no AI system has yet achieved the broad flexibility and everyday understanding displayed by humans across a wide range of domains.

Psychologists often describe human intelligence as a combination of many different abilities rather than a single trait. Research in artificial intelligence has therefore focused on several key components of intelligence, including learning, reasoning, problem solving, perception, and language use. Machine learning, one of the most important branches of AI, enables computers to improve their performance through experience rather than explicit programming. By analyzing large volumes of data, machine learning systems can identify patterns, make predictions, and adapt to changing conditions. These capabilities have led to breakthroughs in fields such as image recognition, speech processing, and recommendation systems.

Natural language processing (NLP) is another major area of AI research. NLP allows computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. Modern AI systems use advanced neural network architectures, including transformers, to process text with remarkable fluency and contextual awareness. These technologies power applications such as chatbots, virtual assistants, language translation services, and automated content generation tools. Recent large language models have demonstrated the ability to answer questions, summarize information, write essays, and even assist with programming tasks, significantly expanding the practical applications of AI.

Deep learning, a subset of machine learning, has played a central role in the rapid progress of artificial intelligence during the twenty-first century. Deep learning systems use multiple layers of artificial neurons to extract increasingly complex patterns from raw data. This approach has dramatically improved performance in computer vision, speech recognition, and natural language understanding. The success of deep learning has been fueled by advances in graphics processing units (GPUs), access to massive datasets, and improvements in training algorithms. As a result, AI systems now perform many specialized tasks at or above human expert levels.

The growing adoption of AI has created both opportunities and challenges. AI technologies are used in healthcare, finance, transportation, scientific research, and education, offering the potential to improve efficiency and accelerate innovation. At the same time, concerns have emerged regarding privacy, algorithmic bias, misinformation, transparency, job displacement, and long-term societal impacts. Researchers, policymakers, and industry leaders continue to debate how AI should be regulated and governed to ensure that its benefits are widely shared while minimizing potential harms. As AI capabilities continue to advance, understanding both its technical foundations and ethical implications remains increasingly important.
